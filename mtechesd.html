    About MTech ESD

    The M.Tech degree in Electronic Systems Design (ESD) is intended to help address the growth of the highly skilled manpower in Chip Design and Embedded System Design needed to support electronics system design and manufacturing (ESDM) in our country.
    The course, in alignment with the national and state policies attempts to foster growth and entrepreneurship in ESDM as outlined by the Department of Electronics and Information Technology (DIETY) of the Government of India, and the Government of Karnataka, respectively.

    12 bit 40 MSPS Pipelined ADC

    System on Chip



    Description: This project implements a 12-bit 40 MS/s pipeline ADC ADC on a 0.18 m CMOS process. The ADC has to be designed together with caring non-idealities. Such structure can be constructed in analog-to-digital converters to make the performance faster. By considering the non-ideal cases for the design, more realistic outputs are expected. There are several non-ideal effects that lower the ADC performance such as gain error and capacitor mismatches. By using calibration techniques, non-ideal effects can be reduced. In this project, .Sampler, MDAC(1.5 bits/stage) were used to construct the ADC and calibration techniques were employed to improve the performance.

    Wakeup Signal Detection circuit for PAM4 signal

    System on Chip



    Description: This project presents a approaches for the receiver circuitry for PAM4 wake-up signal detection housed in the high-speed SerDes receiver block. The front-end analog circuitry is designed in voltage-mode, Voltage mode makes use of a hysteresis voltage comparator for signal detection to detect thethe PAM4 signal at 5GHz.

    Development and analysis of Ventricular Assistive Device

    Embedded System Design



    A minimally invasive Ventricular Assistive Device prototype is designed to assist in the pumping of the heart by inflating and deflating a balloon wrapped around the heart. The inflation and deflation cycle of the balloon is setup in synchronous to the ECG signal via a real time feedback subsystem. The real time feedback unit is designed in a view to promote blood flow in phase with that of the varying ECG signal, based on the heart activity of the user. The designed prototype was verified with a pressure sensor and signal analysis was performed to further verify the working of the design.
